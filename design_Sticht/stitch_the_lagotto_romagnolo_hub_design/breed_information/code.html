The Italian Truffle Dog

Discover the Lagotto Romagnolo

An ancient water retriever from Italy, the Lagotto is now world-renowned as the premier truffle hunter, prized for its intelligence, affection, and iconic curly coat.

history

Origin & History

The Romagna Marshlands

Dating back to the 15th century, the Lagotto (meaning "lake dog" in the local dialect) originated in the marshlands of Romagna, Italy. Initially bred as water retrievers, they worked alongside hunters to fetch waterfowl from the dense reeds of the lagoons.

As the marshes were drained in the 19th century, the breed's role evolved. Their exceptional sense of smell was redirected from ducks to the elusive white and black truffles of the Italian hillsides, making them the only breed specialized for this task.

Care & Grooming

wash

The Hypoallergenic Coat

The Lagotto does not shed, making them excellent for allergy sufferers. However, their hair grows continuously and can mat if not properly maintained. Regular trimming is essential every 6-8 weeks.

fitness_center

Active Lifestyle

They require moderate daily exercise. Long walks, swimming, and especially nose-work or "hide-and-seek" with treats will keep them physically and mentally satisfied.

warning

Pro Grooming Tip: Never brush a dry Lagotto coat; it causes frizz and damages the curl pattern. Always use a detangling spray and brush before a bath.
